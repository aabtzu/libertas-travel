LIBERTAS

A travel planner named after the Roman goddess of liberty.

The Inspiration

In ancient Rome, travelers would invoke the protection of Abeona and Adeona, twin goddesses who watched over journeys. Abeona (from abeo, "I depart") guarded those setting out, while Adeona (from adeo, "I return") ensured their safe homecoming.

According to the Roman author Varro, statues of these goddesses stood alongside Libertas, the goddess of freedom, on the Aventine Hill, signifying that true liberty includes the freedom to journey forth and return as one wishes.

What is Libertas?

Libertas is a trip planner. Drop in a confirmation, paste a Google Maps link, or chat your way through a new itinerary; it builds the day-by-day, plots the map, and lets you share it. The LLM sits behind the seams (parsing, classifying, geocoding) rather than as a chatbot wrapper.

Create via chat

Describe a trip and it builds the day-by-day for you.

Import bookings

Parse PDFs, .ics files, booking emails, and TripIt or Google Flights links.

Explore venues

Search restaurants, hotels, and attractions by city and add them straight to a trip.

Interactive maps

Every stop gets geocoded and plotted on a Leaflet map.

Edit in chat

Add stops, move days, swap hotels, all from the sidebar.

Share three ways

Full itinerary, recommendation list, or written narrative.

Write-ups

One-click readable narrative with links and a map, good for sharing with friends.
