HOW IT WORKS

Stop juggling confirmation emails, notes apps, and browser tabs. Libertas pulls your whole trip into one place — automatically.

Ready to try it?

Create a free account
1

Import your bookings

You've already done the hard part — booking the flights, hotels, and restaurants. Libertas reads what you've got. Upload a confirmation PDF, paste the text from a booking email, drop in a Google Flights link, or just describe the trip in chat. The AI pulls out all the details automatically: dates, times, locations, confirmation numbers.

PDF confirmations Booking emails Google Flights / TripIt links Plain text or chat Spreadsheets
2

Your itinerary takes shape

Everything organizes itself into a clean day-by-day timeline. Flights, hotels, restaurants, activities — each with times, notes, and confirmation details. Switch between a timeline list, a grid, or a calendar view depending on how you like to think about a trip.

Not happy with something? Use the chat panel to adjust: add a stop, move an activity to a different day, or drop in a note about a reservation.

3

See it on a map

Every location in your itinerary gets plotted automatically. Useful for spotting when two things you've booked are on opposite sides of a city, or for getting a feel for the geography of an unfamiliar destination before you leave.

4

Discover what's nearby

The Explore tab gives you access to a curated list of restaurants, hotels, and attractions worldwide. Ask in plain English — "good sushi near my hotel in Tokyo" or "Michelin-starred restaurants in Lyon" — and get recommendations you can add directly to your trip.

5

Share and travel

Share a link with anyone traveling with you. Works well on mobile — your single reference once the trip has started. No app to install, no account required to view a shared trip.

Export to calendar if you want your itinerary in Google Calendar or Apple Calendar. Or just keep it in Libertas — it's designed to be useful when you're on the ground.
